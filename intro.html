Introduction


What is Augmented Sociality?

Due to the resurgence of VR headsets heralded by the Oculus Rift and the use of AR apps and games like Pokemon Go, people are getting closer into Virtual reality and Augmented reality. They sound similar, and as the technologies develop, they bleed over into each other a bit. However, the fact is that they are very different technology with characteristics that readily distinguish them from one another.

Augmented Reality

Augmented reality (AR) is an interactive experience of a real-world environment where the objects that reside in the real world are enhanced by computer-generated perceptual information, sometimes across multiple sensory modalities, including visual, auditory, haptic and somatosensory.

AR VS VR

Virtual reality and augmented reality do two very different things in two very different ways, even though the devices themselves are similar in design. VR aims in replacing reality and taking you to somewhere else. Augmented reality adds to reality, projecting information onto what you already see. They are powerful technologies that have yet to make an impression on consumers, but have shown great potential. They could completely change the way we use computers in the future, but it is hard to say whether one will succeed or both.
